  3. I've used the Tigi Bed Head wax stick for about 7 years now. I used to use and like the LA Looks wax stick, but I believe it is discontinued. I was looking for a cheaper alternative to the Tigi, so I thought I'd give this a try. I use it on hair on my hair, which is 1\\" at its shortest and 2 1/2\\" at the longest. While this is half the price, it just doesn't meet my needs. First, it is much softer than the Tigi and leaves heavy streaks of wax in my hand that then need to be worked in by hand. With the Tigi, I never had to touch my hands and get the wax all over them. Second, this isn't as pliable as the Tigi when set. It does hold more firmly, which is OK, but if I need to fix my hair for any reason I have to wet it down again. One poster complained about the smell. It does have a stronger smell than other products, but it reminds me of a barber shop and I kind of like the smell. Also the smell doesn't linger after applied. Overall, I think the product is good for certain people, but it just doesn't meet my needs. If you have a flattop or aren't as particular as me about the pliability of the product or getting product on your hands, this will probably be a good fit for you.
