  1. I have owned about a dozen of these exact same mirrors in the past ten years. They are regularly available at Steinmart for approximately $15.00, which is still not cheap for a hand mirror. I couldn't resist the far superior clarity of both the 10X mirror and the regular mirror; I've never found any other that could compare to the excellent quality of these mirrors. I also like the U-shaped handle ~ so space-saving and convenient to hang on a hook on the wall inside bathroom linen closet by my makeup shelf. The great aspects of this hand mirror is why it's so disappointing that the plastic holder always, ALWAYS breaks at the folding hinge, usually within the first 3 months. I hoped it was just a fluke but experience has taught me better. At first, the handle will remain firm so it's easy to use either as a hand mirror or to fold into a stand-alone mirror. Then the mirror begins to flop at the hinge. This makes it necessary to hold the mirror around the plastic circle rim. Very awkward and inconvenient. Storage advantage is also lost. The plastic is brittle and soon breaks at the most vulnerable point: the hinge. The plastic around the hinge of my most recent mirror crumbled and fell away so quickly, I'm totally fed up and searching for a new mirror option. I stumbled across this ad on Amazon and felt this serious flaw needs to be shared. For what it's worth, the black plastic one is a little more durable, but I will never buy another of these mirrors. There are plenty of discarded broken ones in a closet basket that I can hold by the rim for a long time to come! Buyer beware because now you know.
