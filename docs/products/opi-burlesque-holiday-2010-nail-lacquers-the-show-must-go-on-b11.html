  1. I bought the Little Teasers set from Burlesque and tried each polish individually. I am in love with this polish! I was eager to try it as soon as I noticed that there is a distinct gold undertone in this primarily hot pink/magenta sparkly polish. Like the other polishes in this set, it has a microglitter that imparts shine rather than sparkle, and when you hold your hand in one direction the color is completely different than it is when your hand is held in a different direction. This calls to mind the paint on some cars that looks different when the car has passed. I hesitate to call this Hot Pink because a.) I don't like hot pink and I like this color and 2.) it is not startling in the least. This is why I call it hot pink/magenta. the color is deeper than your typical hot pink but there is no suggestion of purple in this color at all, so it isn't magenta either. The color goes on with less pigment than I would have anticipated; in fact it is quite sheer with only one coat and streaky. The second coat gives you a good idea of what the color will look like, but three coats were really needed to get the color that the bottle shows. While the polish appears to have that gold undertone, it is better described as seeming a deep orange at the right angle. It also appears a deep red at a third angle. (The picture of the bottle above shows it pretty well in my opinion, but the nails in that picture can't show the multi-tonality of this polish.) It is truly a stunning polish and I will be buying a full-size bottle as soon as this small bottle begins to run out. Removing any of these polishes with the microglitter is a little more time-consuming than your standard polish. However, it doesn't seem to stain the skin as badly as other, more pigment-intense colors.
