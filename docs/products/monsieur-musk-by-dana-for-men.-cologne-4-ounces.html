I read a review of it in Playboy that said it smelled slightly different on every male that wears it. When I started dating the woman who would eventually become my wife in 1975, she used to tell me that both of her two roommates would continually smell the arms of the chair I was sitting in when I left their apartment. Sometime in the late 80s, Houbigant closed its doors. Apparently, Dana bought out much of the company and started selling the product as Monsieur Musk. The first several orders I received were from either New York or Laval, presumably from the old Houbigant houses. Then, sometime in the late 90s, Dana moved to somewhere in PA. Throughout this period, the scent was reasonably close and not yet too diluted. At some point in the late 90s I bought twelve 8oz bottles of cologne from the PA Dana. By this time I had become skin sensitive and quickly developed contact dermatitis to other scents. So, literally, I was hooked on Monsieur Musk. I still have a couple of those bottles, but I have amassed a number of 4oz bottles over the past 12 years. Now, most of the Monsieur Musk I find is made in China under license from Dana. The new stuff smells quite a bit different than the cologne made by Dana in PA, and a lot different than the original. It is also less fragrant. However, it still does not produce dermatitis and I still use it daily as an underarm deodorant (it is great for this, and contains no aluminum). Even today, women at checkout counters, etc, compliment me on my cologne and want to know its name and where they can get it. If you are a long time user, you might want to look for 8oz bottles on the internet. They sell for as much as $80/bottle, but all the ones I have found are still from the PA Dana. The other thing you might look out for is the Chinese spray 4oz bottles that have the removable atomizer. Now I stock up on the 8oz bottles and just decant the 8oz bottles into the Chinese 4oz bottle with the removable atomizer. I am ordering a couple bottles of this Amazon product now, just to see what I actually get. Update (05/25/2017): Beside the considerable dilution of the scent since it has been bottled in China, a major aggravation of the product was the black paint/ink that covered the bottle. The paint/ink is very soluble in alcohol, and the majority of the liquid in the bottle is SD alcohol. When using the splash on product, it is unavoidable that the liquid goes on your hands, and some remains after you &#34;splash on&#34; the fragrance. Touching the bottle with fragrance wet hands causes the paint/ink to dissolve leading to a hard to clean black mess. Furthermore, any of the product that drips down the bottle will leave a very hard to remove black stain at the bottle's base. I was pleasantly surprised when my most recent order arrived. The product has been updated. No longer is the bottle painted/inked black. The bottle is now clear glass except for an apx. 2&#34; black label that bears the information for the product. Also, the ink/paint that is the label does not dissolve when wet with the fragrance. This is a major improvement in the packaging and represents much more than a cosmetic overhaul. As for the fragrance itself, my subjective opinion is that there has been a further dilution. It is not enough to change my 5-star rating, but I do feel I am using a bit more than I was with even the last batch I purchased. Still, Monsieur Musk is a unique fragrance, and I continue to receive occasional compliments for wearing it. Also, I have been using the new bottle long enough to notice if the contact dermatitis I develop within a couple days following use of other fragrances developed, and it has not.
