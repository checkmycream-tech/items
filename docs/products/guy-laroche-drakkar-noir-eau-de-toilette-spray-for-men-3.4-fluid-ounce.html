  29. This might be the quintessential 80's cologne. Since I was a little kid during the 80's you'd think I'd be nostalgic about this stuff, but actually I never smelled it until I was over 30. I have to say though, I like this a lot. Its a big, powerful 80's fougere (That's &#34;fern&#34; in French, but the truth is ferns have no scent, so its the name the French gave perfumes base around a citrus-lavender-vanilla combination, which in modern men's colognes often morphes into citrus-lavender-sandalwood/cedar). This is a big 80's scent all the way, which is why some people love it and why others hate it. That is to say, while most sellers claim this is a combination of &#34;citrus, lavender, sandalwood, and spicy berries&#34; (whatever those are), Guy Larouche actually lists 15 (Yes 15!) different notes: Basil, Rosemary, Green Mint, Verbana, Lemon (top), Angelica, Wormwood, Juniper, Coriander (heart), and Oak Moss, Patchouli, Cedar, Pine Needle, and Balsam (base). So this stuff is kind of like a scent stew where the perfumer took every nice smelling thing he could think of and threw them all in because he couldn't bear to leave anything out. On the one hand, I don't know about you, but my nose simply isn't up to the task of picking out 15 separate notes, so Drakkar Noir is something of an Olfactory cacophony to me. On the other hand its a pleasant cacophony. I get kind of a citrus and freshly showered vibe up front, followed by something that you could kind of describe as &#34;spicy berries&#34; I guess. The drydown is piney and makes me think of a slightly sweet smelling evergreen forest floor, which I actually really like. I didn't own a full sized bottle when I first reviewed this, but I do now because it can be applied after a swim in a chlorinated pool, which cannot be said of either of my regular daily wearers. I don't like this as much as Cerruti 1881 (which is similar but better, more sophisticated, and more restrained imo), or Azzaro Pour Homme, but I do still like it a lot. I also now see why some people hate this though it IS easier to over apply than anything I've used myself. Once I forgot this wasn't Cerruti and applied as many sprays as I do for Cerruti and then instead of the &#34;spicy berry&#34; cacophony that eventually dries down into a nice pine, I smelled like burning tires all day. But if you go easy on the trigger I feel this is a nice scent, and given the amount you should apply it should last a very long time at a reasonable price.
