I've also used the precision trimmer, which is great for maintaining the edge of your beard line, though you wouldn't want to use it for a large area. The nose and ear trimmer is likewise effective, and the fact that it can be washed is important for hygiene and \\"ick\\" factor. Battery life is excellent, even with turbo use. It charges up to full in a little over an hour, and maintains charge for as long as I've ever gone between trimmings (2 weeks or so) and probably longer than that.<br /><br />So what don't I like? The aesthetics and usability of the design. Starting with one of the worst packaging atrocities I've ever seen, with the myriad parts displayed in a plastic shell that will surely scatter them all over once you manage to get it open. Then there's the matter of graphics: who thought black-on-black markings are appropriate for minute adjustments? The length scale on the combs is virtually impossible to read, so I go by number of clicks. It would have been easy to add a white or red indicator line so you could actually see what you're doing, but style (or idiocy) won out here. Ever heard of contrast? No - a fact you'll appreciate when you try to find a specific (solid black) attachment in theprovided all-black bag. There's nothing to do but dump the contents out and look for the item you want, because absolutely everything is black. Considering how much thought and care went into the engineering of the thing, it's truly appalling how little went into design and usability. This is one category where the Braun beats out the Norelco handily, but then Braun has a long history of great industrial design. But still, overall this one's a winner. Not perfect, but very, very good.
