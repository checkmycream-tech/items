  7. For context, I have skin that is dry, sensitive and also acne prone. I love using masks to mitigate my skin issues - drying masks for clearing up breakouts fast and hydrating masks to reduce the affects of aging and make my skin feel soft. I decided to try the Fresh Black Tea Instant Perfecting mask because I have a friend who absolutely raves about it. She was a convert after a salesperson tried it on her at Sephora and continued to love it after getting a full sized container. Currently my favorite masks are anything by GLAMGLOW (especially [[ASIN:B00ISEOY20 GLAMGLOW Thirstymud Hydrating Treatment, 1.7 oz.]] and [[ASIN:B009GYVMAS GLAMGLOW Super-MudTM Clearing Treatment 1.2 oz]]) when I can splurge and [[ASIN:B0083S44LM First Aid Beauty Ultra Repair Instant Oatmeal Mask-2 oz.]] when I'm looking for something a little less expensive. I was curious to see how this would compare. The first thing I noticed was the smell. I can't quite pick out what it smells like but it is not as pleasant as the GLAMGLOW products or First Aid Beauty. It smells less like an added fragrance and more like an active ingredient. If you're expecting something intentionally pleasant smelling, you might be disappointed. The experience of having it on your skin is interesting. Applying it to my face it smelled quite creamy and rich. However there is an interesting tingling/cooling sensation when I put it on that I wasn't expecting. It reminded me more of how some of the gel masks feel on my face like [[ASIN:B000A0ADUW Ole Henriksen Blue Black Berry Enzyme Facial Mask, 3.5 Fluid Ounce]], though that product doesn't tingle quite as much. It isn't painful or unpleasant, but was definitely unexpected. Thankfully this isn't a mask that hardens or itches so it is very pleasant in that way. After removing it it does make my face feel moisturized and healthy. I think it does a really nice job at this though I also have pretty reasonable expectations when it comes to skincare. I expect them to earn their keep, but I'm not expecting a miracle cure. Comparing this with the other products I've mentioned I do think from a moisturizing perspective it's better than the First Aid Beauty Mask, but not quite as powerful as the GLAMGLOW Thirstymud. Thankfully, the tingling sensation goes away a few minutes after you take it off and it doesn't seem to increase my skin's sensitivity to other products. I really, really like this mask, but ultimately I like the GLAMGLOW Thirstymud better. To me it is more moisturizing, smells better, and feels nicer on my skin. However, the Fresh Black Tea Instant Perfecting Mask, while having a higher price, is actually cheaper per ounce since you get 3.3 ounces in a container vs. GLAMGLOW's 1.7. (Not sure why the product name says 3.4 because this is actually 3.3 ounces.) So it's worth thinking about it price is an important consideration for you, especially if you don't mind the smell or a little tingle. Ultimately it's a really good mask even if it isn't a clear winner for me over other luxury masks on the market.
