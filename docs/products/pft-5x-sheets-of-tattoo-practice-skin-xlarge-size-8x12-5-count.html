Also I wanna add a note about stencil transfers.<br /><br />I found a different/possibly better way to transfer stencils on these fake \\"skins\\" if you can call them that. While most of us are used to using tattoo pens, or felt tips the rubber skins take lead from mechanical pencils quite well, standard No. 2 graphite pencils work but for some reason not as well. I use 0.7mm basic lead refills.<br /><br />Paper; Notebook and printer paper will work find if you take your eraser on the back side of the paper and rub your eraser over the back side of your drawing. However I use Calco/Calque Tracing paper that is semi-transparent, you can pick this stuff up just about anywhere. Walmart is where I get mine. The tracing paper will allow you to transfer the lead drawing with ease. I clean my skin first and make sure it's dry, and lay down my drawing obviously lead-down and take my finger and just gently rub the backside to press it against the skin. No wash or solution with drying time needed like with Ink.<br /><br />I found this by accident and want to share this with everyone since I've been doing the painful Ink method for a while and it's a pain in the neck. The ink smears easy on the rubber skins and that's assuming it actually stays on the skin in the first place. The lead won't smear, and stays on the skin better than you'd imagine.<br /><br />You can clean off your lead transfer by using Vaseline and cloth.
