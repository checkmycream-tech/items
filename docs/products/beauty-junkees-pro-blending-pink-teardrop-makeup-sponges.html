  57. Now that I've had a chance to use this sponge (let's call it the Pro Sponge) as well as the Original BB, I have the following thoughts:<br /><br />Texture: The Original BB is spongier and has more spring to it. The Pro Sponge is firmer, including at the tip.<br /><br />Size: The Original BB is smaller, both dry and when wet, although it expands more when wet than the Pro Sponge. The Original BB also dries more quickly than the Pro Sponge.<br /><br />Performance: I tried both sponges with cream foundations (Kevin Aucoin Sensual Skin Enhancer, Tarte Amazonian Clay 12 Hour Foundation) and a liquid foundation (Armani Luminous Silk). All were used over Hourglass Veil Primer. With thicker formulations, like creams, the Pro Sponge performed better than the Original BB. The firmer nature of the sponge allowed it to force thicker products to blend into the skin. Whereas with the Original BB, the spongier nature made it more difficult to spread thick product. Further, because of the overall firmer nature of the Pro Sponge, the tip was also firmer, making it easier to get in the crevices around the eyes. With liquid formulations, it was a close call, but the Original BB seemed to do a better job. The softer, bouncier texture was able to distribute the runnier foundation very smoothly. However, I had to look VERY closely to discern any difference, and once I applied a setting powder, that difference all but disappeared.<br /><br />Recommendation: Given the price difference, and the superior performance of the Pro Sponge for thick formulations, I would wholeheartedly recommend the Pro Sponge over the Original BB. It's a more versatile product in my view, and obviously much less expensive. I would also mention, however, that both sponges do absorb more product than a brush does, and I would probably choose to use a brush for most liquid formulas. Where the Pro Sponge really shined was in the application of VERY thick formulas like the Kevin Aucoin Sensual Skin Enhancer, which if you're not familiar with it, is thick enough to be used as a very full coverage concealer. Brushes leave this formula looking cake, unevenly spread, and as if it's still sitting on top of the skin. The Pro Sponge did and excellent job of forcing the product around and blending it into the skin itself.
