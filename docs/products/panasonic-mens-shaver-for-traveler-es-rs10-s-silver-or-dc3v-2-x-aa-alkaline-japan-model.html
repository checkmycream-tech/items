UPDATE: After some time of use, I think this is better than the ES3831K and is one of Panasonic's best battery operated shavers, possibly because of the &#34;Made in Japan&#34; quality, even if it is only a dry shaver. If you get the version from Hong Kong rather than Japan (which is the same shaver and still Japanese made) it has English writing rather than Japanese on the back of the shaver. Apparently, according to others the carrying case made the Sanyo more bulky, because this is a very compact shaver and doesn't take up much room, so perhaps the carrying case would not have been as useful as I first thought. It would have been nice to include a case as it is optional to use, although I'm not sure how the new Panasonic cleaning brush (not the same as the Sanyo) that I really like would have fit into the case. I have read in some places that it has an aluminium body, however it feels like plastic, so it may be incorrect information. A good idea is to periodically oil the shaver once you have cleaned all of the hair out, by applying three or so drops of baby oil made from mineral oil AKA liquid paraffin or pariffinum liquidum (check the ingredients, don't use other types of lubricating oil because it is not good for the skin and will leave a scent) onto the foil while it isoperating. Don't oil the motor or inside the shaver as it will cause damage to the shaver and will gum up the motor. In the instruction manual (from memory, I don't have it with me) I think it mentions using a small bottle of oil obtained from Panasonic to oil the shaver, but this is not available outside Japan and is only liquid paraffin (baby oil.)
