  19. So I just got the nail polish today and tried it out. The picture does not equate to the actual coloring of the polish. On the picture you'll see sort of maroon colors with a lighter and darker colors mixed in with a curving motion. Not how it works in real life, as most things go. The polish itself is a bit more burgundy and the swirling effect with colors tends to darken rather than lighten, at least in my case. Also, and very important (it took me three tries before figuring this out), you have to use the magnet immediately after you paint a nail. And I mean, a nail. Its not like you can paint them all and then go back and have it swirl, no you have to paint one nail and immediately grab the magnet and hold it over. The magnet they sent on the polish top didn't even work so I had to go and grab one off the fridge which worked much better. Also, the directions tell you to put one coat, have it dry than add another coat and then use the magnet. I found that using two coats didn't work in creating the magnetic effect. I ended up using only one good coat and it swirled much better, but like I said I used a fridge magnet. I painted one nail and immediately grabbed the magnet and held the whole thing over my nail for a couple of seconds, any longer and it just didn't create the effect as well. You also won't get the same effect on every nail, each nail is different I've found. The polish is a nice coloring and it you're not in a hurry, doing the magnet process can be cool if it works out but over all, to me (and I do have the time to do it) I just don't think its worth the price especially since it doesn't really work out as well as is pictured. I'm saving up to get the Gelish products and nail dryer so hopefully once I get all those products than maybe the Gelish polish will work better than the regular polish.
