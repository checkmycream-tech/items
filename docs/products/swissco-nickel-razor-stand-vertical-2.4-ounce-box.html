  36. One could reasonably ask, \\"What makes a well designed razor stand? For me, there are several universal standards of good design that apply to all products of this sort. First does the design accomplish its functional goal? A razor stand should hold the razor in a stable position that protects both the razor and the user and accomplish this in an ergonomically efficient manor. Surprisingly this small lightweight stand accomplishes this job with flying colors. I originally worried that it would not be as stable enough, but the added mass of the metal loop and the cup for the razor stem combined with the well balanced cantilever design create a very stable low center of gravity. Without my glasses, I have worse than 20-400 vision, yet even without them and with wet hands, I have never once had trouble placing the razor securely in it's stand. Next the item must accomplish its functional goal with a minimum amount of material. More is less. This rule is exemplified in the minimalistic lines of the Swissco stand. There is nothing missing and nothing superfluous. It is strong enough to withstand potential mishaps and light enough to minimize damage to anything if it should be dropped. Next, there is nothing about the design that permits for arbitrary stratification by wealth. There is no optional gold finish or monograms. Finally, age should not add or detract from the value. The nickel finish will neither corrode nor develop a \\"wonderful patina\\". I can expect my stand to be as attractive and functional 20 years from now as it is now. There is no bonus for having the latest and greatest improved model, and it doesn't require any special care. It doesn't need to be polished, buffed, towel dried or stored in its own special box. All in all, while it would be easy to buy a lesser stand at various price points, you could not buy a better stand at any price.
