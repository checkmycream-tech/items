I seem to remember the original formula having more warmth, more heat, and think it's been tweaked slightly to it's detriment at some point about ten years ago---- yet nonetheless, I am so glad the this perfume, which came frighteningly close to being discontinued, has remained in production and become easier to find. This has been a favorite of mine since I discovered the scent over ten years ago. If you are curious about the head, middle, and base notes of the perfume the information is pretty easy to find online, though a definitive formula is and should be elusive. To me, the raspberries and pepper stand out the most, but it is a complex carefully crafted scent that is sweet and hot in a way that is sophisticated yet fun. While I describe it as sweet and hot I nonetheless don't think it's overly sensual or sexy - I think it's complexity drives it into a middle ground between a sophisticated day scent and a sensual evening scent. It's fun but it's not tawdry. It's very nearly transcendent. Overall, I'd say that it comes off as kind of eccentric in a glamorous, intelligent way... and isn't that the spirit of wearing haute couture? I try other perfumes here and there, but I always come back to Hot Couture. I like to think of it as my personal signature, but I also think a few more people could stand to try this perfume and see if it is for them... not all perfumes suit all people, there are plenty of good perfumes, to each their own, and so on ... but I know that I want to keep wearing this for a long time.
