This is a surfactant that is present in almost every shampoo and liquid skin cleanser in America, including those made by Mastey, supposedly a leader in the field of unusually gentle, healthful shampoos. Mastey includes cocamidopropyl betaine in some of their products: On my bottle of Traite, it's in the list of ingredients, right after their much-touted sulfoalkyl amides and esters. In 2004, cocamidoproply betaine was named \\"Allergen of the Year\\" by the American Contact Dermatitis Society, noting its importance as an 'emerging allergen' for children. If you experience irritation of the scalp or face and can't figure out what's wrong, it's worth considering that CAPB in your shampoo might be the culprit, especially if you have sensitive skin. Yet, when you go shopping, just try to find one single shampoo, even a baby or 'natural' shampoo, where this stuff is not listed among the first three or four ingredients. In the American marketplace, there are only three or four shampoos without CAPB, and it's necessary to go to health food stores or Amazon to find them--if you know their names.
