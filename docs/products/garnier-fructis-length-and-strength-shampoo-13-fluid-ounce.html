  1. I don't usually write reviews for beauty products, because the results are so subjective. My experience with Garnier Fructis Length & Strength shampoo, though, was too dramatic to keep to myself. Some quick history: I have straight, baby-fine hair. Fortunately, there's a lot of it on my head because unfortunately, a good handful of hair usually comes out every time I wash my hair. Never thought much of it -- shedding hair is normal, amounts vary greatly, I'm not bald or anything so I figured that was just my thing. As I've gotten older (I'm in my 40's now), it's gotten worse but not enough to worry me or anything. I just kinda figured as I got older, my hair was getting less-resilient and, I guess, \\"brittle\\" is the word I used in the title, here. It didn't *feel* like it was snapping off or anything, but the evidence was clogging my drain. I didn't consider this an issue I could do anything about, and after some experimenting with hair boosting treatments like Ookisa, Samy Fat Hair, and Thicker Fuller Hair -- with varying and not-terribly-remarkable results -- I resigned myself to whatever drugstore \\"volumizing\\" shampoo caught my eye, alternated with a clarifying or moisturizing or some kinda non-volume shampoo every week or so, just to mix things up. I ran out of my non-volume shampoo a few weeks ago, and snagged this Garnier Fructis \\"Length & Strength\\" with absolutely NO expectations -- didn't even read the back of the bottle, that's how casually I chose this product.
