Based on great reviews online, I decided to try this shampoo and conditioner for my color-treated hair after I really damaged my ends experimenting with the ombre look, didn't like it, and colored back over it right away. To their credit, these products have a great fragrance that is not too heavy and is very likable. Not flowery or fruity, it should work for both men and women. My disappointment is that it doesn't leave my hair manageable at all. The shampoo is okay, but seems to take a lot of it to feel like it is really cleaning. The conditioner gets an F for detangling. I can barely comb out my hair, necessitating use of a leave-in product, which I don't like because I can easily get oily. If I glop on a ton of the conditioner, then I can comb through it, but it is too expensive to be using so much each time. Besides, the brush still will not glide through as I am blow-drying, again necessitating use of some leave-in product. By the way, my hair texture is fine and it is cut in layers with the longest part being maybe 3 inches past my shoulders. These products are specifically for fine hair, so I expected better results. I'm also not sure if I can verify any color protection from this shampoo and conditioner. I did a touch-up on just my root area and after the first washing with the Wella shampoo and conditioner, it had lightened a bit. I feel that some of the inexpensive brands I've tried from the drugstore can give me the manageable hair I want, so I would not purchase the Wella again.
